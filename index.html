Why It Matters

Why Estate Planning Is Important

Without an estate plan, state law determines who receives your assets, who manages your affairs, and, in some cases, who will care for your children. These decisions may not reflect your wishes.

Protect Your Family

Control how your assets are distributed and protect your spouse, children, and future generations.

Avoid Unnecessary Probate

Reduce delays and expenses, and designate trusted individuals to make financial and healthcare decisions if needed.

Plan for Incapacity

Estate planning isn't only about death — it's about being prepared, not just for the end, but for life's unexpected turns.

“Most people already have an estate plan — it's called the state's default plan. The question is whether it's the one you want.”
