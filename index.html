Why It Matters

Without a plan, the state decides for you

Estate planning is not just about what happens after you pass away — it is about protecting yourself, your family, and the assets you have worked hard to build throughout your lifetime.

  • Control how your assets are distributed
  • Name guardians for minor children
  • Designate trusted decision-makers for incapacity
  • Avoid unnecessary probate delays and expense

“Most people already have an estate plan — it’s called the state’s default plan. The question is whether it’s the one you want.”

Our Services

Estate planning, tailored to your family

There is no one-size-fits-all estate plan. We take the time to understand your family, your goals, your assets, and your concerns before recommending a planning strategy.

Revocable Living Trusts

Flexible, trust-based estate plans that can help avoid probate and manage assets during incapacity.

Wills & Pour-Over Wills

Clear instructions for asset distribution and estate administration, coordinated with your broader plan.

Powers of Attorney

Durable financial and healthcare powers of attorney so trusted people can act if you become incapacitated.

Healthcare Directives

Living wills and medical powers of attorney that guide loved ones and providers during emergencies.

Probate Avoidance

Strategies to help reduce or avoid the time, cost, and public exposure of probate court.

Trust Administration

Guidance for trustees and families coordinating trust funding and administration over time.
